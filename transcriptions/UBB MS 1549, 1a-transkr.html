The text is from the matins of Botulph (17 June), with nine proper lessons. The fragment was a gift to the Bergen museum by cand. theol. H. Daae in 1829. Missing or unclear text has been supplied by the transcriber from the Acta sanctorum Iunii III, Antverpiæ 1701 (1969), p. 402-403.

The fragment is quite worn, especially on the verso-side. Unclear text is not marked as such when the letters are identifiable with use of a text source. Smaller holes in the parchment are not noted in the transcription.

Lesson six

The victorious and blessed father Botulphus tirelessly walked the regions abandoned and deserted by men, carefully considering all the places around him. Finally he decided upon Ikanho with God’s guidance, to establish a monastic community there, after the suitable buildings had been erected. But this was a wasteland, and after it was left by the Christians it had been possessed by demons. Their deceiving ghosts had to be chased away by the arrival of the many soldiers of Christ, and the pious life of the faithful be introduced, so that this place formerly abounding in the devil’s deceit should abound in the grace of the merciful creator.

Lesson seven

Upon the arrival of our blessed teacher Botulphus, terrible fumes rose from the ground. Since the whole world is illuminated by your miracles, why did you have to uncover our darkness? You act inhumanely and disregard all piety by driving us poor wretches away also from this deserted region, we who have been chased from all the rest of the world.
